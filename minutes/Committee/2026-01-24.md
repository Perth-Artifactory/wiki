### Curtin Robotics Club (CRoC)

We recieved the following from Silas Butler on 2026-01-06.

> Hi Artifactory Committee,
>
> We're beginning to do our sponsorship rounds and wanted to confirm whether the Artifactory would be keen to continue to sponsor CRoC going into 2026. The Artifactory has been a long standing supporter of our club, helping get events like our Hackathon off the ground as well as lending their time and expertise. 
>
> If you would be keen on continuing to sponsor us, I think it would be good to rehash some of the details with our new executive team, to provide clarity on the terms of the sponsorship that may not have been written down.
>
> Kind Regards,
>
> Silas Butler
> Executive Committee - Secretary

We need to decide:

* Is our relationship with CRoC worth continuing?
* If so, what is each side offering?

Previously, we had offered some combination of:

* Sponsored membership for selected CRoC representatives
* Access to tools and training
* Discounted or waived tool usage fees for CROC projects
* Time and attention of our volunteers to facilitate use of the workshop and tools, outside regular opening hours

The benefits we obtained were:

* Advertising the Artifactory within the Curtin community
* Personal satisfaction of members interested in CRoC's success
* Association with CRoC's good works (e.g. teaching 3D printing to PNG teachers.)

The costs and risks we incurred were:

* The time and attention of our volunteers
* Damage to multiple expensive tools (IR reflow oven and 3D scanner) that seem to be associated with CRoC member usage (and which were not reported by the user at the time) - unresolved.
* Poor compliance with induction procedures (e.g. visitor induction, visitor signup / indemnity waiver) that exposed us to risk.
* Poor oversight of CRoC users in the workshop generally.

Discussion:
* Lewis Yip:
  * I wasn't directly involved with CRoC so happy to be corrected on anything below.
  * I think our experience with CRoC last year (and with uni students generally) is that they have less experience and ability to make good decisions than our general members.
  * They are interested in achieving their goals, but not really interested in becoming members, sustaining the space, engaging with our processes, or treating our tools nicely.
  * The nature of what CRoC wants from us (intensive use of our workshop and our high end, expensive tools for their club projects) needs a lot of volunteer effort to manage appropriately.
  * Simultaneously, our volunteers who assist with CRoC are not necessarily equipped to play "bad cop" and enforce our policies and procedures, especially when there are multiple CRoC people and only one volunteer.
  * If we do engage with CRoC again, I think we need to control their use of the space much more tightly, to prevent further tool damage and risk exposure.
  
Lewis Yip - ACTION - Advise CROC that we will not be continuing sponsorship this year.
